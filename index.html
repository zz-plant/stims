Lineage

Credit the form clearly.

Stims follows the lineage of Ryan Geiss’s MilkDrop without pretending to be an official continuation or a full compatibility clone.

What carries forward

  • Preset-driven feedback visuals and fast visual iteration.
  • Browser-native access to preset browsing, editing, import, and export.
  • Clear credit to MilkDrop, Winamp-era context, and preset authors.

What changed

  • The browser is the runtime, so setup and compatibility have to be explicit.
  • The site is no longer pretending to be a general toy catalog.
  • The canonical launch surface is a dedicated route instead of a query-heavy homepage.
