Structure

The split is deliberate.

Stims still lives in the lineage of MilkDrop, but the browser version is easier to understand when setup, playback, and editing stop overlapping.

What setup owns

  • Quick compatibility checks before users commit to playback.
  • Audio source choice: demo, mic, tab capture, or YouTube-backed tab capture.
  • Device tuning such as quality presets, safe mode, and motion settings.

What live playback owns

  • Preset browsing, favorites, recent history, and collection-based discovery.
  • Autoplay, random runs, transition control, and runtime inspection.
  • Live editing plus import, export, duplication, and deletion for editable presets.

Why this reads better

The product gets simpler when the surfaces stop competing.

No mixed setup

Setup lives in one place.

Users should not wonder whether to choose audio on the homepage or inside playback.

No second browser

Preset work happens live.

The homepage can preview presets, but the real browser stays inside playback.

No premature editing

Editing follows motion.

Users see the visuals first, then edit while the current preset keeps running.
