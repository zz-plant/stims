Before you start

Compatibility and performance

Stims starts fast with demo audio, then adjusts to your browser and hardware. This page tells you what to expect before you dive in.

Open Stims Browse looks first

Best case

Recent laptop or desktop browser

This is where the richest motion, color, and layering tend to show up, especially on newer hardware with stable graphics drivers.

Expected fallback

A lighter pass is still the real thing

If Stims eases back on rendering load, the goal is steady motion and responsive controls, not a degraded experience.

Fastest first run

Start with demo audio

Demo audio avoids permission friction and gets everything moving immediately. Microphone and tab capture are optional upgrades.

What to expect

Browser and device guidance

Browser guidance
Setup Expectation
Newer desktop browser Richest detail and the best shot at the heaviest rendering stack.
Older laptop or integrated graphics Stims may ease back to keep motion stable.
Phone or tablet Expect a cleaner, lighter run with touch-friendly controls.
TV-style browser Use the default quick looks first and keep extra tuning minimal.

When things look simpler

What fallback really means

  • Stims may trim detail to keep motion smooth.
  • The animation should still feel expressive and reactive.
  • You can keep using the app without digging through technical settings.

Quick fixes

If the first run feels rough

  • Use demo audio first instead of microphone or tab capture.
  • Open Look and try Battery saver or TV balanced.
  • Enable the steadier-hardware toggle inside Advanced tuning.

First steps

The easiest way in

  1. Open Stims and press Play with demo audio.
  2. Switch through a few looks until you find one you want to stay with.
  3. Only then move to your own mic, room, tab, or YouTube audio.
